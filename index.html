Plus Address Factory

What? Why?

Some email providers (gmail, fastmail) allow you to create variants of an email address with plus addressing.

For example, the address john+foobar@gmail.com goes to the mailbox john@gmail.com.

When you sign up for a service, take advantage of plus addressing to create a unique email address that you only use for that service. This allows you to:

  • Use unique email address to mitigate credential theft, in the same way that you should use a unique password with every site.
  • See if a service provider is leaking your address to other companies.
  • Blacklist known comprimised addressed.
  • Easily filter your inbox.
